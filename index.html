In Honor Of

Dr. Abdourahamane Diallo

Physician, Statesman, and Pioneer of the First Guinean Republic

Born in Dalein, Labé, Dr. Abdourahamane Diallo was a foundational pillar of Guinea's independence. As a brilliant physician and a core architect of the PDG, he dedicated his life to building the infrastructure of a free Guinea.

Political & National Service

1958

Minister of Rural Economy

Appointed to Guinea's very first independent cabinet following the historic "No" vote to France.

1963

Minister of Health

Leveraged his medical background to build and transition the nation's healthcare system from scratch.

1968

Secretary of State

Served as Secretary of State without portfolio, remaining a trusted advisor in the inner circle.

Historical Timeline

February 1955

The Political Foundation

Becomes one of the five core members of the high-level executive bureau of the PDG alongside Sékou Touré.

October 1958

Independence Achieved

Guinea declares independence. Dr. Diallo steps up as an essential technocrat to fill the administrative void left by departing colonial forces.
